A B O U T

I'm a fourth-year at UC Berkeley majoring in Bioengineering with a minor in Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences. Within my major, I am pursuing the biomedical devices concentration and am particularly interested in control systems, 3-D modeling, biomechanics, biomaterials, and robotics. Currently, I am doing research in the Biomimetics Millisystems Lab at UC Berkeley, working on the design and fabrication of novel electrostatic actuation and its applications. Previously, I have conducted research on the relation between brain activity and complex spatial and acoustic behaviors using echolocating fruit bats as the model species.

I'm also a member of the Berkeley Forum, a non-partisan organization that brings a diverse range of speakers and their ideas to campus.

In my free time, I enjoy hiking, fishing, and cooking. I'm a huge fan of Arsenal Football Club and usually spend my weekend mornings watching their games.
